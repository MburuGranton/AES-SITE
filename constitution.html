A.E.S Constitution

Article I: Name

The association shall be known as "THE ASSOCIATION OF ENGINEERING STUDENTS - THE TECHNICAL UNIVERSITY OF KENYA CHAPTER".
The association’s office and premises shall be located within the institution of the TECHNICAL UNIVERSITY OF KENYA.

Article II: Objectives

  1. To nurture innovation in students and purpose the exposure to technological ideas.

  2. To act as a link between A.E.S members and I.E.K, K.E.T.R.B and E.B.K. It will also promote cooperation with the professional bodies and the organization as may become necessary from time to time;

  3. To promote industrial interactions between AES members and industries in and outside Kenya.

  4. To promote academic linkages between AES members and other associations and institutions in and outside Kenya.

  5. To establish and maintain funds on such terms as may be arranged from time to time by such executive committees for projects aimed at achieving the association’s objectives.

  6. To offer mentorship in terms of student-student mentorship and student-professional mentorship.

  7. To participate in climate change, environmental and wildlife conservation.

  8. To advocate for better mental health and well-being for its members.

Article III: Membership

The membership of the association consists of three categories:

Full Membership

This is open to bonafide students only duly admitted to the Technical University of Kenya and registered as engineering students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Associate Membership

This is open to any student undertaking any other course within the Technical University of Kenya or a course in engineering studies outside the Technical University of Kenya.

Honorary Membership

This is open to any past members of the Association or any person who has rendered valuable service to the association.

*Note: Full membership is open to any engineering student of the Technical University of Kenya, the membership fee being Ksh. 200/=. The registration fee for associate members shall be Ksh. 500/=

RIGHTS AND PRIVILEDGES
  1. General Meetings

    (i) A full member may seek election to any position of office (except final year students), join in requisitioning a General Meeting of the Association and attend any General Meeting and speak there at subject to the provision of this constitution.

    (ii) An associate member may only attend and speak at any General Meeting of the Association but shall neither be reckoned with when determining whether or not there is a quorum at such a meeting nor may he/she join in requisitioning any meeting of the Association nor be elected to any office unless the General Meeting unanimously permits such a state of affairs.

    (iii) An honorary member may only attend and speak at any General meeting of the Association but shall neither vote nor be reckoned with when determining whether or not there is a quorum at such a meeting of the Association nor be elected to any office unless a state of affairs is unanimously permitted at such a General Meeting. They may only be part of the advisory committee.

  2. Any member intending to resign from the Association shall submit in writing to the Secretary General which takes effect from the date of receipt by the Secretary General of such a notice.
  3. Any member may be expelled from membership if the committee so recommends and if a general Meeting of the Association shall resolve by 2/3 majority of its members that such a member be expelled on the grounds that his/her conduct had adversely affected the reputation or dignity of the Association, or that he/she has contravened any of the provisions of the constitution of the Association. The committee shall have power to suspend a member from his/her membership until next General Meeting of the Association following such suspension but not withstanding such suspension a member whose expulsion is proposed shall have the right to address the General Meeting at which his/her expulsion is to be considered.
  4. Any person who resigns or is removed from the Association shall not be entitled to a refund of his/her subscription or any part thereof or any moneys contributed by him/her at any time.
  5. Notwithstanding anything contained within this constitution, any member who falls into arrears with his subscription for more than six months or is removed from such a membership shall automatically cease to be a member of the Association and his name shall be struck off the register of members and can only be returned provided that the committee may, as its discretion, reinstate such member on payment of the subscription outstanding.
Article IV: Patron

The patron lends credibility and support to the association.

  1. The patron shall be proposed by the General meeting and senior academia in the institution and shall preferably be an engineer, technologist or scientist in the field of engineering.

  2. The patron shall work closely with the Executive Committee to coordinate the activities of the Association with those of the Engineering profession with an aim to attaining the association’s objectives.

  3. The patron shall assume office immediately after his/her election and he/she, unless he/she declares his/her intention to contrary, continues in office for such period shall deem fit.

Article V: Office Bearers

The committee shall consist of:

  1. President

  2. Vice President

  3. Secretary General

  4. Finance Secretary

  5. Publicity Secretary

  6. Organizing Secretary

  7. Head of Secretariat

  8. Project and Workshop Coordinator

  9. Gender Secretary

Article VI: Duties of Executive Committee

The office bearers shall have the authority and responsibility provided for in this constitution;

  1. The President

    1. Preside over all meetings of the committee and at all general meetings, unless prevented by illness or other sufficient cause.

    2. Be a co-signatory of all the finances and other negotiable instruments.

    3. Ensure proper and cordial relations among various organs of the committee and the professional bodies.

    4. Has no voting right unless in the event of a tie- and is the only full member with no voting right.

    5. Shall work to safeguard and implement the general policy of the Association.

  2. The Vice President

    There shall be a vice president and shall be of opposite gender with the president:

    1. In absence of the president, assume their roles and responsibilities and those outlined by the constitution.

    2. Shall be in charge of registering members.

    3. This office will be responsible for auditing the association’s accounts.

  3. Secretary General

    1. Shall deal with all the correspondence of the Association under the general supervision of the Committee in cases of urgent matters where the committee cannot be consulted; the secretary general shall consult the president. The decisions reached shall be subject to rectification or otherwise at the next executive meeting.

    2. Shall be responsible for keeping minutes of all such meetings and for preservation of all records of the proceedings of the Association and the committee.

    3. Shall be in charge of keeping the records of registered members.

  4. Finance Secretary

    1. Shall receive and shall also disburse, under the directions of the committee, all moneys belonging to the Association and shall issue receipts for all money received by him/her.

    2. Shall be responsible to the committee and to the members that proper books of accounts of all moneys received and paid by the association are written up, preserved and available for auditing.

  5. Publicity Secretary

    1. Shall act as the Public Relations officer and shall work to further the interest of the Association.

    2. ii. Shall work in close conjunction with the Secretary General’s office.

  6. The Organizing Secretary

    1. Shall be in charge of coordinating events of the association

  7. The Head of Secretariat

    1. Shall be in charge of all secretariats

  8. Project and Workshop Coordinator

    1. Shall see to the academic pursuit of the members of the Association by preparing trips, lectures and seminars or in his/her absence the organizing secretary.

    2. Shall also act as the academic secretary of the Association.

  9. Gender Secretary

    1. Shall ensure that all genders are well represented in the AES.

    2. Shall also oversee and be in charge of the well being of AES members.

Article VII: Procedure in General Elections

  1. The elections board shall be constituted by the executive committee

  2. General elections for membership of the Association shall take place at least one month before the end of the academic year.

  3. All association elections shall be by secret ballot, simple majority voting system.

  4. Whenever there is a tie in any association elections, there shall be a second ballot, third etc, until a candidate shall obtain a simple majority.

  5. No new member of the Association shall stand for elections unless he/she has been a member for at least 4 weeks before the election date.

  6. Notice of the Association elections shall be posted by the Returning officer.

  7. The notices shall state the date fixed for elections and shall call for nominations.

  8. Closure dates for the receipt of nominations by the Returning Officer shall be fixed in the notice of election.

  9. The closing dates in (g) above shall be at least 7 days from the date of the posting of the notice and 7 days from the holding of the election.

  10. The nomination forms for the president must contain the names, admission number and signatures of at least 100 proposers and that for other members of executive committee must contain names, admission number and the signatures of at least 50 proposers. All proposers must be fully paid up members of the association.

  11. The Returning Officer shall prepare and publish a statement showing the persons who have been nominated

  12. The Returning officer shall publish notice of the poll on all students and the Faculty of engineering notice boards and shall arrange for the preparation of ballot papers, suitable accommodation as polling stations, and for a member of the academic or administrative staff of the University and a student representative to be in attendance at each polling station.

  13. The officers in attendance at the polling stations shall act as counting agents on closure of polling.

  14. The Returning officers shall seal the ballot boxes in the presence of all candidates on the day of each election, before the time at which polling is due to begin, and cause the sealed boxes to be delivered to the officers in charge of each polling station.

  15. Only returning officers, counting agents and candidates shall be present at the counting of ballot papers.

  16. The returning officer shall announce the results of the counting on its completion to the candidates there present, and shall order a re-count if so requested by any candidate in the event of a tie.

  17. Any decision as to what shall be regarded as a spoilt paper shall be taken by the returning officer whose decision shall be final.

  18. The returning officer shall post a notice of the results of the election as soon as is possible after the counting of the votes.

  19. In case of unopposed candidate on any post the returning officer shall declare the candidate to be validly nominated and elected.

  20. Aspirants shall obtain nomination forms upon payments of non refundable nomination fee of Kshs. 500/=

  21. Final year students shall not be eligible to contest for any elective post.

  22. An aspirant for the post of the president must have served in any AES committee in any year preceding the elections. That notwithstanding the election board shall have the power to vet any aspirant for nomination

Article VIII: Duties of the Executive Committee

  1. The executive committee shall be responsible for the management of the Association and for that purpose may give directions as to the manner in which, within the law, they shall perform their duties. The executive committee shall have power to appoint such committees as it may consider being desirable to make reports to the executive committee upon which such action shall be taken as seems to the executive committee desirable.

  2. All moneys disbursed on behalf of the Association shall be authorized by the executive committee and supported by 2/3 of the committee members.

  3. The quorum for meetings of the executive committee shall be not less than 2/3 of the members. Resolutions of the committee shall be decided by a simple majority rule. In the event of a tie, the president shall have the deciding vote.

  4. If an executive committee member ceases to hold office by reason of resignation, death or by incapacity arising from mental or physical illness or by a vote of no confidence passed against him/her at an executive committee meeting and supported by 2/3 of the members of the committee, a by-election shall ensue within six weeks. The procedure shall be the same as in A of this Article VII.

Article XI: General Meetings

There shall be two classes of general meetings: Annual General Meetings and Special General Meetings.

  1. The annual general meeting shall be held not later than the 6th week of the 1st Semester in each academic year. Notice in writing of such meeting accompanied by the statement of account (see ARTICLE XII (A)) in the agenda for the meeting shall be sent to all members not later than 21 days before the date of the meeting and, where applicable, by Press advertisement no less than 14 days before the date of the meeting.

  2. The agenda for any annual general meeting shall consist of the following:

    1. Confirmation of the minutes of the previous annual general meeting

    2. Consideration of the accounts

    3. Election of office bearers and the committee members (and trustees where necessary in accordance with Article XI (a).

    4. Such other matters as the committee may decide or so as to which notice shall have been given in writing by a member or members to the secretary at least four weeks before the date of the meeting.

    5. Any other business with the approval of the President.

A special general meeting may be called for any specific purpose by the committee. Notice in writing of such meeting shall be displayed on the faculty of engineering notice boards not less than three (3) days before the date of the meeting.

Article X: Procedure at Meeting

  1. At all meetings of the Association, the President, or in his/her absence, the Vice President shall take the chair.

  2. The President may at his/her discretion limit the number of persons permitted to speak in favor of and against any motion.

  3. A resolution shall be decided by simple voting by a show of hands. In the case of equality of votes, the President shall have a second or casting vote.

Article XI: Auditor

  1. An auditor shall be appointed for the following year by the annual general meeting. All the Association’s accounts, records and documents shall be opened to the inspection of the auditor at any time. The finance Secretary shall produce an account of his/her receipts and payments and a statement of assets and liabilities made up to a date which shall not be less than six weeks and not more than three months before the date of the annual general meeting. The auditor shall examine such annual accounts and statements and either certify that they are correct, duly vouched and in accordance with the law or report to the Association in what respect they are found to be incorrect, unvouched or not in accordance with the law.

  2. A copy of the auditor’s report on the accounts and statements together with such accounts and statements shall be displayed at the annual general meeting. An auditor may be paid such honorarium for his/her duties as may be resolved by the executive committee.

  3. No auditor shall be an office bearer or a member of the Association.

Article XII: Funds

The funds of the association may be used for the following purposes:

  1. All money and funds shall be received by and paid to the Finance Secretary and shall be deposited by him/her in the name of the society in any bank or banks approved by the committee.

  2. No payments shall be made out of the bank account without a resolution of the committee authorizing such payment and all cheques on such bank accounts shall be signed by the finance secretary, the President and the Patron of the Association.

  3. A sum of not exceeding Ksh. 1,500/= may be kept by the finance secretary for petty disbursement of which proper account shall be kept.

  4. The committee shall have power to suspend any office bearer who it has reasonable cause to believe is not properly accounting for any of the funds or property of the Association and shall have power to appoint another person in his/her place. Such suspension shall be reported to a general meeting to be convened on a date not later than two months from the date of such suspension and the general meeting shall have power to decide what further action should be taken in the matter.

  5. The financial year of the Association shall correspond with the academic year of the Faculty of Engineering.

Article XIII: Editorial Board

The editorial boards shall be constituted to publish the Association of Engineering Students magazine. The board shall be instituted within the first three meetings of the executive committee after the elections.

  1. Composition

    The board is composed of the following:

    • Editor-in-chief

    • Managing editor

    • Not more than four editors

  2. Appointment

    1. The elected committee shall appoint from among the fully paid up members and must consist at least a first or second year student

    2. In the appointment of a new board, priority shall be accorded to continuing student members of the outgoing board

General Guidelines: Code of Conduct

  1. All board members will be expected to strictly adhere to specific timelines on any assignment.

  2. Meetings are a compulsory session of the board functions and will always be communicated in advance. Timely attendance of meetings as communicated will be expected.

  3. Any member who fails to attend four consecutive meetings without a sound cause or fails to perform his/her responsibility will be recommended to the executive committee for dismissal from the board.

  4. Common sense shall prevail at all times.

Responsibilities
  1. Editor-in-chief

    1. Responsible for all editorial and journal activities on behalf of the editor board in the interest of the board.

    2. Reports to the AES executive every month on the progress of the board relevant and related issues regarding the magazine.

    3. Has the deciding vote on any contentious issue during the board meetings

    4. Communicates dates and chairs the board’s meetings.

    5. Recommend members for dismissal to AES executive with approval of other board members.

  2. Managing Editor

    1. Assumes responsibilities of the chief editor in his/her absence.

    2. Provides advice on expenditure and the financial management to the board.

    3. Prepares the board’s budget for approval by the executive committee.

  3. The Board

    1. Sourcing for advert, sponsorship and articles

    2. Editing the articles.

    3. Drafting the board’s work plan or activities.

Terms of Service

  1. The executive committee shall serve for one academic year.

  2. The term of the executive committee shall expire 2 weeks before the new elections.

  3. The board shall handover all records and assets to the executive committee upon the expiry of their terms.

  4. A commission of 10% of the value of advertisement or any other value that may be approved by the executive committee will be awarded to the person(s)who source(s) the advertisement .

Article XIV: Amendments to the Constitution

  1. Any legible member of the association may propose an amendment to this constitution by bringing to the office of the vice chairperson an amendment motion. They cannot , however, be implemented without prior consent in writing of the Registrar, obtained upon application to him/her made in writing and signed by three of the office bearers.

  2. A committee of students chaired by the Vice President approved by two thirds of the members shall review and amend the constitution.

  3. Amendments to the constitution of the Association must be approved by at least a two-thirds majority of members at a general meeting of the society.

Article XV: Dissolution

  1. Association shall not be dissolved except by a resolution at a general meeting of members by a vote of two thirds of the members present. Notice of this meeting shall be displayed on the faculty of engineering notice boards at least 14 days before the date of the meeting.

  2. No dissolution shall be affected without prior permission in writing, to the Patron, obtained upon application to him/her made in writing and signed by three of the office bearers.

  3. When the dissolution of the Association has been approved by the Patron, no further action shall be taken by any executive committee member of the Association in connection with the aims of the society other than to get in and liquidate for cash all the assets of the society. Subject to the payment of all the debts of the Association, the balance thereof shall be distributed in such other manner as may be resolved by the meeting at which the resolution for dissolution is passed.
